## Steps in a Data Analytics Process

![The CRISP-DM Process for Data and ML](https://almirgouvea.github.io/The-Crisp-DM-Methodology/_static/crisp-dm.png)

To define step-by-step a professional's  workflow we can refer industry standards like *"CRIP-DM"*. Now this is a semi-comprehensive framework but you can mix and match accroding to your needs.

>"A data analyst doesn't just stare at spreadsheets. We follow a detective process. Today, we are going to do this live in rapid-fire rounds."

1.  **Define the Problem:** What economic assumption are we testing?
2.  **Collect Data:** Gathering the raw evidence.
3.  **Clean Data:** The 'digital janitor' phase so the math doesn't crash.
4.  **Model & Analyze:** Finding the mathematical signal.
5.  **Storytelling:** Translating the math back into human reality.


So all that sounds cool but how can we touch these steps right here right now to get a taste for the process.

Okay, so what is the dataset for our analysis?To prove that data analytics is about real human behavior, we aren't going to use a dusty, pre-packaged spreadsheet from 2014. We are going to analyze the most interesting dataset in this room: You.Today, we are going to look at the "Attention Economy" and test some fundamental microeconomic theories—like opportunity cost and resource constraints—using your actual digital habits.We will be looking at:Human Capital Constraints: Your Current CGPA, Living Situation (Hostel vs. Day Scholar), and Average Nightly Sleep.The Attention Budget: Your Total Screen Time from yesterday and Total Device Pickups.App-Level Utility: The exact apps where you spent the most time.Please Join the Survey linkScan the QR code below on your phones. It will take you to a quick, anonymous Proofgrad survey.(Insert QR Code Image Here)Don't overthink it, just pull open your device settings (Screen Time for iOS, Digital Wellbeing for Android) and drop the numbers in. You are actively generating the data we are about to analyze.Let's see some interesting insights(This is where the live analysis happens!)Now that we have the data flowing in, let's do what data professionals call "Exploratory Data Analysis" (EDA). Before we build complex models, we need to understand the baseline of our ecosystem.The Attention Baseline: What is the average screen time of a Manipal Economics student?The Push vs. Pull Market: We can plot Notifications Received against Device Pickups. Are you opening your phone because an app pinged you (external market trigger), or are you opening it purely out of habit (internal utility)?Utility Shifts: Do 1st-semester students spend their time on entirely different apps than 6th-semester students who are nearing graduation?Try to fit some hypothesis and modelsNow we move from observation to testing. In economics, you learn about "Homo economicus"—the perfectly rational decision-maker. Let's see if the data supports that.We are going to test the Opportunity Cost of Attention. Every hour you spend in the digital market is an hour you aren't spending accumulating human capital (studying).Let's run a live Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression right here on the screen:$$\text{CGPA} = \beta_0 + \beta_1(\text{Screen Time}) + \epsilon$$Does a higher screen time mathematically correlate with a lower CGPA? We will let the regression line tell us the truth.Maybe run some evaluationsA careless data analyst stops at the first correlation. A great data analyst looks for confounding variables.Remember, correlation does not equal causation. If we see a negative trend between screen time and grades, we have to ask: is the screen time causing the lower grades, or is there a hidden factor?Let's introduce Sleep into our model.Often, we find that it isn't the screen time directly hurting the GPA. Instead, excessive screen time destroys sleep quality, and the lack of sleep is the biological constraint that tanks academic performance. This is called "Omitted Variable Bias," and identifying it is why human analysts are still more valuable than automated charts.Wrapping Up: Your Toolkit for the FutureYou don't need to be a software engineer to do this.
